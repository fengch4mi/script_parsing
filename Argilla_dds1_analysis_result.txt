Argilla is a complex and deeply human character, despite the non-human
circumstances she finds herself in. Her journey, as revealed through these
quotes, is one of profound moral conflict, emotional intensity, and a
desperate search for meaning and a return to a lost sense of self.

## Character Analysis: Argilla

### Personality:

1.  **Compassionate and Empathetic:** This is Argilla's most defining trait,
and it constantly clashes with the brutal reality of her world.
    *   She expresses immediate concern for others: "Remember that," when
        discussing leaders; "Serph, we can't leave Jinana. Let's rescue her."
    *   She abhors "needless killing" ("Why...? I don't want to. I don't like
        needless killing.") and is deeply sickened by the act of devouring: "I
didn't... eat those people. I couldn't have!", "No, I... I can't eat them."
and "How could we possibly deserve something like this!?"
    *   Her tears for Jinana ("Argilla's tears fall upon Jinana's cheek") and
        her offer of help ("There's a girl at our base who can help you
control this power... Come with us... Okay?") highlight her deep capacity for
empathy and desire to alleviate suffering.
    *   She expresses concern for Sera's well-being multiple times, ensuring
        she's eaten and protecting her.
    *   Her internal musings "Why do I feel like I must protect my comrades?"
        show this protectiveness is a fundamental, almost instinctual part of
her.

2.  **Loyal and Protective:** Argilla deeply values her comrades and
leadership.
    *   She asserts "a leader is irreplaceable" and expresses solidarity:
        "We're comrades, am I right?"
    *   Her fierce defense of Jinana ("Serph, we can't leave Jinana. Let's
        rescue her.") and rage over her death ("Our comrade died!") underscore
this loyalty.
    *   She is quick to stand up for her allies, even against her own, as seen
        when she threatens Gale ("I'll stop you if you even try it.") for
suggesting fighting Jinana.
    *   She takes responsibility for her actions, lamenting "What should I do?
        It's all my fault..." after being tricked by Bat.

3.  **Fiery and Temperamental:** Argilla possesses a powerful temper that
erupts when she's stressed, angered, or cornered.
    *   Her actions speak louder than words: "Argilla fires her gun in rage,"
        "Argilla slaps Heat across the face," and "Argilla grabs Gale in
anger."
    *   Her verbal outbursts include "Don't.", "Stop. Be quiet.", "Shut up!",
        "Heat, no! you can't trust that traitor!", and "Are you joking! Bat
makes me sick!"
    *   This rage is often a direct response to perceived injustice, betrayal,
        or the loss of comrades.

4.  **Curious and Investigative:** She seeks understanding and explanations
for the bizarre phenomena around her.
    *   "Who could she be?," "I want an explanation," "Something happened
        here. I want to know what that was."
    *   She questions the nature of their existence: "What's "cursed?" Who is
        this "god?"", "What's really going on? How can we understand...?" and
"What exactly is the Karma Temple? Where did it come from?"

5.  **Practical and Observant:** She pays attention to details and assesses
situations.
    *   "I have never seen anything of that nature."
    *   "Something is wrong. There are no guards."
    *   "These traps are pretty elaborate."
    *   She often points out strategic concerns ("We're outnumbered," "They're
        going to trap you by using Sera as a shield").

6.  **Vulnerable and Overwhelmed:** Despite her strength, she is often
overcome by physical weakness and emotional distress.
    *   "My chest is on fire," "I have not felt well since we returned...", "I
        feel... so weak..."
    *   Her moments of despair ("What's the point, then? Why live like this?
        Why are we even alive!?") reveal a soul struggling to cope.

### Psychology:

1.  **Deep Moral Conflict and Disgust:** The core of Argilla's psychology is
her visceral rejection of the "eat or be eaten" reality. She's sickened by the
forced cannibalism (even if demonic) and violence. This moral code clashes
violently with her survival instincts and the laws of the Junkyard, causing
immense psychological strain and self-loathing. She views the Atma
transformation as a "curse" and desperately wants to be "normal" again.

2.  **Existential Crisis and Search for Identity:** The sudden transformation
and the revelations about their past (being devoured, dying, coming from
Nirvana) shatter her sense of self and purpose.
    *   She questions the meaning of life: "Why live like this? Why are we
        even alive!?"
    *   She struggles with fragmented memories: "I feel like I have met her
        before," "I've heard that song somewhere," "I remember blue, too..."
This disjointed recall of a past life adds to her confusion and her desperate
need to understand her true identity and the nature of her existence.
    *   The idea that she was "devoured" before is deeply disturbing,
        challenging her very understanding of continuity and self.

3.  **Trauma Response and Emotional Outbursts:** Her outbursts of rage and
despair are not just personality quirks but psychological responses to the
traumatic events she endures: the forced transformation, the violence, the
loss of comrades, and the constant threat of being devoured. She is clearly
suffering from the psychological toll of her environment.

4.  **Desire for Redemption and a Better World:** Her drive to find a "cure"
and reach "Nirvana" is fueled by a desire to escape the suffering and
violence, not just for herself but for everyone. She envisions Nirvana as a
place where "Nobody will suffer like this anymore," indicating a longing for
peace and humanity.

### Development:

1.  **Initial Resistance and Disbelief:** Argilla begins as someone horrified
by the new reality, denying her part in the devouring and feeling physically
ill. She clings to the hope of a cure ("I'll do anything to find a cure for
this curse!") and a return to "normal."

2.  **Forced Adaptation and Moral Compromise:** As events unfold, she
reluctantly accepts alliances that "don't seem right" ("...but so be it") and
understands the necessity of fighting and even devouring for survival, though
she never truly embraces it. This is a painful shift, driven by circumstance
rather than conviction. Her compassion, however, remains her guiding
principle, making these compromises deeply unsettling for her.

3.  **Intensified Emotionality and Loyalty:** The death of Jinana marks a
significant turning point. This loss galvanizes her, deepening her grief,
strengthening her resolve to protect others, and fueling her anger against
those responsible (Bat, Mick). Her tears for Jinana and her subsequent
confrontational actions show a character whose emotions are raw and whose
loyalty has been profoundly cemented by shared suffering.

4.  **Emergence of Memories and Existential Dread:** As more hints of a past
life emerge, Argilla's internal struggle intensifies. The possibility of
having died and been "devoured" before, and the implications for Sera, push
her into a deep existential crisis, questioning the very fabric of her reality
and purpose. This newfound knowledge makes her more desperate for answers and
a permanent solution.

5.  **Determined Search for Truth and Resolution:** Despite her lingering
suffering and doubts, Argilla ultimately resolves to face their "destiny" and
uncover the truth in Nirvana. She shifts from merely wishing for a cure to
actively pursuing the path that promises understanding and an end to the cycle
of violence, even if it means confronting harsh truths about her past and the
nature of her existence. Her compassion remains, but it's now tempered by a
fierce, weary determination to protect her comrades and forge a better future.
She understands the necessity of power (even if she dislikes how it's
acquired) to achieve this greater good.

In conclusion, Argilla is a character defined by her profound humanity in an
inhuman world. Her journey is a testament to the resilience of compassion and
loyalty in the face of overwhelming horror, as she desperately seeks to
reconcile her inherent goodness with the brutal demands of survival and
unravel the twisted reality she inhabits. Her constant questioning, emotional
vulnerability, and fiery determination make her a deeply relatable and
compelling figure.
